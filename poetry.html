i am

i am the wasp performing their Sisyphean algorithm against the murky bus window, too lost in desperation to bother with intimidating passengers, still garnering distrustful glances.

Always Passing

I'm always in passing, always passing away.

Dancing in worlds of things known and unknown, what could and could never be.

Waltzing through worlds I created, the worlds that created me.

Gracefully swaying with them until ruin

I meant to plant a garden,

tilling the plots, and

preparing to sow

But instead I merely plant

another self six feet below.

My regrets are a corpse's whisper on the wind.

I exorcised my devils,

pinned and placed them

under glass frames

I forsook my gods and

painted Jackson Pollocks

with my Darling's brains.

I've died often enough, the graves I rob are mine

I have casually set myself

down on curbs and walked

away as someone new.

I wordlessly left my first and second loves and ghosted a

town a time or two

I have left so many; so many times.

I'm always in passing, always passing away.

The Burden of Perceptability

I wish to be seen, but as a zoo creature is seen.

You may relate to my silly antics, but surely you will never understand me.

I want people to arrive with interest, but be cautioned not to spook me.

Hiding places are readily available and invading my space is seen as act of aggression.

Boxes and Boxes of Me

I unpack myself

I spill myself across the floor

Algorithmically, I

fold,

sort,

reduce,

shuffle,

reshuffle,

stack,

reduce,

index,

pin,

arrange,

combine,

reduce,

repack

I separate my realities

I unstitch my atoms

I pretty up my feelings and give them shape and form

And box them up so they can't make me feel any more

Prose to rid me of ideas that hellishly endured

Even here, words where emotions once stood

These small apologies

To be finished

House with a Leaky Faucet

My mind is a house with a leaky faucet

Or it was.

My mind was a one room house with a faucet leaking moment after moment and there was never a drain.

I furnished my house with special interests and naive aspirations and ignored the water lapping at my ankles.

Reflections of the people and locations, my needs and fancies, wallpapered it

My hoarded curiosities and routines brought me more and more stuff as the water left me less and less space

My mind is a labyrinth of the houses it's outgrown like a hermit crab, non-Euclidean, nonsensical, nomadic, trying to outrun the dripdripdrip of living

I can knock down walls, burst doorways and punch holes in the ceilings

I can annex and renovate and decorate

I can construct a web of houses, each with purposeful accommodations, thread together by hallways and secret passages and an ever diminishing sense of identity

And yet I can never reach outside myself, be more than I am

My mind is a Frankensteinian patchwork of houses, attempting to elude the ocean growing steadily within; storing myself in only the last few floors.

As construction continues, I've learned how to live more efficiently;

how to save and how to say goodbye

But each lesson came at a cost,

I've lost keys

I've set rooms ablaze

I've barricaded doorways (purposefully or accidentally) with clutter

I've forgotten whole closets of things in the rush

Sometimes

I dive under the relentless deluge and recover my past

But always

The water claims house after house

Knowing one day it will kiss the last ceiling and we'll call it a life.

My mind is a house with a leaky faucet and I am but the ghost who haunts it.
